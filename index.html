About the campaign

Jaano Haq Se is an initiative by Kanshiram Welfare Foundation with the objective to bridge the gap between the public institutions and the public by providing a common platform to understand their individual rights and the roles and responsibilities of the public institutions in their lives.

The campaign is focused towards mass awareness in the society and building of trust between both the stakeholders of the society. We conduct community level open dialogue sessions where the representative of a public institution and the people from the community engage in dialogue. The representative informs and discusses the institutions’ role and relevance within the society and the services, support and assistance provided these institutions. The people also engage with the representative to understand about the institution by discussing on various aspects and sharing their individual views. This will in turn lead to a higher accountability and transparency of the public institutions. The public institutions that would be approached for the campaign will be those institutions that have a direct impact on the lives of people like educational, healthcare, legal/judicial and social institutions. This process and exercise will help to achieve the idea of welfare state.

We also employ different modes of communication such asstreet plays, workshops and community building programs to put across the information to the people. This will result in an in-depth understanding of both the public about the roles and responsibilities of the various institutions; and the institutions’ understanding of the problems and challenges faced by the people, which in turn helps them to prepare policies and schemes that are more meaningful and relevant to the society at large.

The campaign commenced from Jahangir Puri community area and is henceforth being organized in communities across Delhi.

The overall agenda of the campaign is to empower the people by making them aware of their constitutional rights for greater engagement with public institutions to exercise their role as stakeholders in a democracy. It is the right of every Indian to be aware of the basic rights guaranteed by our Constitution to live a life with dignity.
